Douglas Fir

Scientific Name: Pseudotsuga menziesii

Type: Evergreen Conifer

Average Lifespan: 500–1,000 years

Native Region: Pacific Northwest (including BC)

Care & Maintenance

  • Watering: Every 2–3 weeks (more if young)
  • Soil: Well-drained, slightly acidic
  • Sunlight: Prefers full sun
  • Pruning: Late winter or early spring

Environmental Benefits

  • Absorbs carbon dioxide
  • Supports local wildlife
  • Stabilizes soil
  • Improves air quality

Growth Timeline

  • Years 1–5: Root establishment
  • Years 6–15: Moderate trunk growth
  • Year 15+: Can grow 70–100m tall

Fun Fact: Despite its name, the Douglas Fir isn’t a true fir tree!
