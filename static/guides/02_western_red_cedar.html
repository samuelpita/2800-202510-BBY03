Western Red Cedar

Scientific Name: Thuja plicata

Type: Evergreen Conifer

Average Lifespan: 1,000+ years

Native Region: Pacific Northwest (including BC)

Care & Maintenance

  • Watering: Weekly, especially in dry seasons
  • Soil: Moist, well-drained
  • Sunlight: Full sun to partial shade
  • Pruning: Light shaping in early spring

Environmental Benefits

  • Provides habitat for wildlife
  • Resists rot and pests
  • Improves air quality
  • Used for sustainable building materials

Growth Timeline

  • Years 1–5: Establishing roots and foliage
  • Years 6–20: Rapid vertical growth
  • Year 20+: Can exceed 70m in height

Fun Fact: Known as the "Tree of Life" by Indigenous peoples for its many uses.
